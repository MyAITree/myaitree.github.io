"It is precisely the preponderance of Madhyam — its nyaas bahutva — that bestows on Bhimpalasi its unique depth. When the accent shifts away from Pa toward Ma, the raga blooms into its full afternoon longing." — After MusicMaster; Sangeetapriya; Grokipedia
01Parichay (परिचय)

Raga Bhimpalasi (also Bheempalasi, Bheempalas, Bheempalashree) is one of the most emotionally rich ragas in Hindustani classical music. It belongs to Kafi Thaat and is Audav-Sampurna — five notes ascending, seven descending.

Its defining structure is the omission of Re and Dha in the ascent, both appearing only in descent. Its two komal notes — komal Ga and komal Ni — carry andolan and mandatory kan swaras (grace notes). Ma (Madhyam) is the vadi: the emotional centre everything gravitates toward. An afternoon raga (12–3 PM) evoking the heavy, tender longing of midday.

Re and Dha — the symmetrical approach rule: Re is approached only from komal Ga above (g → Re). Dha is approached only from komal Ni above (n → Dha). Neither note may be reached directly. This symmetrical oblique approach is one of Bhimpalasi's defining characteristics — violating it changes the raga's identity immediately.

04Aaroh–Avroh aur Pakad
Aaroh — ascending · 5 notes · Re and Dha absent .n Sa (Ma)g Ma Pa (Sa)n Sa' ((Ma)g = komal Ga with Ma kan; (Sa)n = komal Ni with Sa kan; Re and Dha are completely absent)
Avroh — descending · all 7 notes · approach rules apply Sa' n Dha Pa Ma g Re Sa (Dha via n: n → Dha; Re via g: g → Re — these approach rules are mandatory every time)
The approach rule — the most important structural feature: In descent, Dha must be preceded by komal Ni (n → Dha). Re must be preceded by komal Ga (g → Re). Jumping directly to Re from Ma, or to Dha from Pa or n, violates Bhimpalasi's grammar. This symmetrical oblique approach is unique to this raga among Kafi-family ragas.

Key melodic rules

  • Re and Dha absent in aaroh — never use them going up
  • Re approached only via g (g → Re) in descent
  • Dha approached only via n (n → Dha) in descent
  • Ma is the vadi and primary nyaas — all phrases return to Ma
  • Komal g takes Ma kan; komal n takes Sa kan
  • Aaroh begins from lower .n — the sweep .n Sa g Ma Pa n Sa' is idiosyncratic
  • Purvanga-pradhan — lower half is the emotional centre

Character and feel

  • The afternoon timing captures the heavy, languid, tender mood of midday
  • Monsoon imagery — gentle rains, afternoon longing — is deeply associated
  • Ma's preponderance (nyaas bahutva) gives the raga its meditative depth
  • When Pa is over-emphasised, the raga drifts toward Dhanashri — Ma must dominate
  • Vilambit khayal in Bhimpalasi is among the most expressive forms in Hindustani music
  • Moves between deep pathos and warm devotion with equal ease
07Alankars (अलंकार)

Practise with tanpura drone. Focus: no Re or Dha ascending; approach rules in descent; Ma kan on g; Sa kan on n; Ma as primary resting note throughout.

ALANKAR 1 — Core aaroh–avroh
.n S (M)g Ma Pa (S)n S' | S' n Dha Pa Ma g Re S (no Re or Dha ascending; n→Dha and g→Re in descent; (M) = Ma kan; (S) = Sa kan)
ALANKAR 2 — Aaroh step (5 notes only)
S (M)g | g Ma | Ma Pa | Pa (S)n | n S' (Re and Dha never appear going up)
ALANKAR 3 — Avroh step (approach rules)
S' n | n→Dha | Dha Pa | Pa Ma | Ma g | g→Re | Re S
ALANKAR 4 — Trikhand aaroh
.n S g | S g Ma | g Ma Pa | Ma Pa n | Pa n S'
ALANKAR 5 — Trikhand avroh
S' n Dha | n Dha Pa | Dha Pa Ma | Pa Ma g | Ma g Re | g Re S

.n S Ma g Pa Ma , g Ma g Re S , .n S g Ma Pa n Dha Pa Ma g Re S (the complete Bhimpalasi pakad — commits the raga's full character to muscle memory)

10Bandish (बंदिश)

11Taan Prakar (तान प्रकार)
All taans must: (1) never use Re or Dha in ascending runs, (2) approach Re via g and Dha via n in descending runs, (3) let Ma dominate as the resting point between sections.
Seedhi taan (straight — full arc)
.n S g Ma Pa n S' | S' n Dha Pa Ma g Re S
Vakra taan
S g Ma g Ma Pa Ma Pa n Pa n S'
S' n Dha n Dha Pa Dha Pa Ma g Ma g Re S
Sapat taan (fast continuous)
.n S g Ma Pa n S' S' n Dha Pa Ma g Re S (note approach rules hold mid-run)
Meend taan (characteristic in vilambit)
g ~~~ Ma – , Ma ~~~ g – , g ~~~ Re – , Re ~~~ S (~~~ = meend; slow glides are defining)
12Bhimpalasi and the Kafi Family

Kafi Thaat is the most crowded thaat — many ragas share similar note sets. Knowing Bhimpalasi's specific distinctions is essential for maintaining its identity in performance.

Bhimpalasi
↑ S g Ma Pa n S' · ↓ S' n Dha Pa Ma g Re S
Varjit aaroh: Re + Dha. Vadi: Ma. Afternoon. Approach rules: g→Re; n→Dha.
Kafi
↑ S R g Ma Pa Dha n S' · ↓ same
All 7 notes in aaroh. Vadi: Pa. Night. No approach restrictions. Lighter character.
Bageshri
↑ S g Ma Dha n S' · ↓ S' n Dha Ma Pa Dha Ma g Re S
Varjit aaroh: Re + Pa (not Dha). Vadi: Ma. Night. Pa vakra in avroh.
Bhimpalasi vs Bageshri — the key distinction: Both have vadi Ma and komal Ga + Ni. Bhimpalasi omits Re and Dha in ascent (Pa present). Bageshri omits Re and Pa in ascent (Dha present). Quickest test: if Dha appears in the ascending phrase → Bageshri. If Pa but no Dha in ascent → Bhimpalasi.

Bhimpalasi vs Dhanashri

  • Both: Kafi thaat, same aaroh structure (Re + Dha varjit)
  • Bhimpalasi: vadi is Ma — Ma dominates
  • Dhanashri: vadi is Pa — Pa dominates
  • Shifting accent from Pa to Ma = Bhimpalasi appears
  • Overemphasising Pa in Bhimpalasi creates Dhanashri — the most common error

Bhimpalasi vs Dhani

  • Dhani is shadav (6 notes) — omits Ma entirely
  • Bhimpalasi uses Ma as the vadi — complete opposite treatment
  • Dhani aaroh: S g Pa Dha n S' (no Ma)
  • Quickest test: if Ma is prominent → Bhimpalasi; if Ma is absent → Dhani
  • Both are Kafi thaat with komal Ga and Ni
13Rasa aur Bhaav (रस और भाव)
Shringaar — yearning
A tender, aching yearning — the slow longing of afternoon, the weight of waiting, love expressed with restraint and depth. Not dramatic but deeply felt.
Karuna — compassion
Bhimpalasi evokes compassion and pathos with unusual directness — the komal notes and Ma preponderance create a sustained heaviness that moves the listener involuntarily.
Bhakti — devotion
Widely used in devotional compositions — its afternoon time aligns with midday prayers, and its depth and serenity are natural vehicles for spiritual surrender.
Varsha — monsoon
Post-1950s compositions draw on monsoon imagery — gentle rains, afternoon longing. The raga's timing coincides with afternoon showers, deepening this seasonal association.

18Practice Schedule

Daily practice — 50 minutes

  • 0–5 min: Tanpura setup; let Ma settle as the gravitational centre of the drone
  • 5–12 min: Alankar 1 — core aaroh–avroh; no Re/Dha ascending; approach rules in descent; kan swaras
  • 12–18 min: Alankars 2–5 — step patterns, trikhand; confirm rules are natural
  • 18–28 min: Alaap phrases 1–3; .n Sa g Ma opening; Ma as primary resting note
  • 28–38 min: Alaap phrases 4–6; n→Dha approach; taar saptak; full resolution
  • 38–44 min: Sargam geet with Teentaal
  • 44–50 min: Meend taans — g ~~~ Ma ~~~ g ~~~ Re ~~~ S; slow and deliberate
DayFocus
MondayAlankars; drill ascending rule (no Re/Dha) and approach rules (g→Re; n→Dha) until automatic
TuesdayAlaap phrases 1–3; .n Sa opening; kan swaras on g (Ma kan) and n (Sa kan)
WednesdayAlaap phrases 4–6; n→Dha approach in context; Ma as constant nyaas swara
ThursdaySargam geet with taal; approach rules within rhythmic constraints
FridayBandish "Ja Ja Re" sthai — learn and memorise; feel the afternoon character
SaturdayBandish antara + full composition; compare Bhimpalasi with Bageshri and Kafi
SundayListening — Parveen Sultana or Pandit Jasraj; ideally in the afternoon hour
19Common Mistakes to Avoid
MistakeCorrection
Using Re or Dha in the aarohCompletely absent ascending. Their appearance instantly turns the raga toward Kafi. The strict aaroh is: .n Sa g Ma Pa n Sa'.
Not approaching Re via gIn descent, Re must always be preceded by komal Ga (g → Re). Jumping from Ma or Pa directly to Re violates Bhimpalasi's most important structural rule.
Not approaching Dha via nIn descent, Dha must always be preceded by komal Ni (n → Dha). Jumping directly to Dha destroys the raga's character.
Skipping kan swaras on g and nKomal Ga takes a Ma kan; komal Ni takes a Sa kan. These are structural, not optional. Without them, the komal notes sound inert.
Overemphasising Pa → becoming DhanashriMa must remain dominant. If phrases keep resting on Pa, redirect attention to Ma. Excessive Pa emphasis creates Dhanashri, not Bhimpalasi.
Confusing with BageshriBhimpalasi: Re + Dha absent in aaroh (Pa present). Bageshri: Re + Pa absent in aaroh (Dha present). If Dha is in the ascending line → Bageshri. If Pa but no Dha → Bhimpalasi.
Rushing through the ragaBhimpalasi's emotional character lives at slow vilambit pace. Begin all practice at the slowest comfortable tempo. Rushing undermines the raga's essential depth.
