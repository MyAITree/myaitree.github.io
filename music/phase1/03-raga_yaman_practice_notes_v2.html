Raga Yaman
राग यमन — Comprehensive Practice Notes
"When we use tivra Ma, it is like a prayer of shantha; and whenever we use shuddha Ma, there is a little bit of shringara — relief, tenderness, arrival." — Ustad Vilayat Khan, on the twin-Ma interaction in Yaman Kalyan
01Parichay (परिचय)

Raga Yaman (also called Kalyan, Iman, Aiman, Eman; Kalyani in Carnatic music) is one of the most fundamental, most performed, and most beloved ragas in Hindustani classical music. It belongs to the Kalyan Thaat and is a Sampurna raga — all seven notes in both ascent and descent. It is typically among the first ragas taught to beginners, yet every great master of the tradition has performed it throughout their career.

Its defining characteristic is the teevra Madhyam (Ma♯ / F♯) — the raised fourth. All other notes are shuddha. This single sharpened note gives Yaman its luminous, expansive quality: open, floating, reaching upward. The raga also features the use of shuddha Ma (F♮) as a relief note in Yaman Kalyan — a cadential arrival point after the teevra, creating the shringaar quality Vilayat Khan described.

Yaman, Kalyan, and Yaman Kalyan: Pure Yaman (Puriya Kalyan) uses only teevra Ma — shuddha Ma is strictly forbidden. Yaman Kalyan (the most commonly performed form) inserts shuddha Ma in specific phrases — typically as a descent cadence Ga Ma♮ Ga Re Sa — while using teevra Ma everywhere else. Most concert renditions, classical recordings, and bandishes labelled "Yaman" are in Yaman Kalyan form. These notes are verified from Parrikar Archive, Ragajunglism, and musicmaster.in.

04Aaroh–Avroh aur Pakad
Aaroh (आरोह) — Ascending .Ni Re Ga m♯ Pa Dha Ni Sā' (Aaroh begins from lower Ni — .Ni → Re → Ga is the characteristic opening sweep. Pa is sometimes omitted: .N R G m D N S')
Avroh (अवरोह) — Descending (pure Yaman) Sā' Ni Dha Pa m♯ Ga Re Sā
Avroh cadence — Yaman Kalyan (shuddha Ma relief) Ga Ma♮ Ga Re Sā (Ma♮ = shuddha Ma, F♮; appears only in this cadential descent phrase — "a little shringara"; in all other positions teevra m♯)
The aaroh signature: Yaman's ascent must begin from the lower Ni (.Ni → Re → Ga). Beginning directly from Sa sounds like a scale exercise, not Yaman. The .Ni Re Ga opening sweep — with a smooth meend — is the raga's melodic signature and should be felt in every alaap phrase.
Pakad (पकड़) .Ni Re Ga Re , Sa , Pa m♯ Ga , Re , Sa (the opening .N R G R establishes the raga; Pa m Ga Re Sa is the descent cadence; Ga is visited repeatedly)
Key characteristic phrases (to practise daily) .N R G R S , R G m♯ G R S , G m♯ Dha Ni S' , S' Ni Dha m♯ G R S (these four phrase cells define Yaman; memorise them before alankars)

Key melodic rules

  • Open from .Ni — aaroh must sweep from lower Ni; never from Sa directly
  • Ga is the vadi and primary nyaas — every significant phrase resolves to or rests on Ga
  • Ni and Ga create the melodic axis — the interplay between these two notes defines the raga's identity
  • Teevra m♯ throughout — pure Yaman never uses shuddha Ma as a resting note
  • Ga m Ga = the defining phrase — Ga both sides of teevra Ma; use it constantly
  • Pa is lightly treated — phrases often skip Pa: G m D N S'
  • Smooth meends essential — Ni → Re and Ga → m♯ are the two critical glides

Character and feel

  • The Lydian mode's "floating" quality is the key — the raised fourth creates perpetual upward yearning
  • Yaman is described as vastly luminous and of infinite possibility — the teevra Ma is its crown
  • Ga as vadi means the raga feels grounded despite the floating Ma♯ — it creates a beautiful tension
  • The .Ni → Re → Ga sweep is one of the most recognisable openings in all of Hindustani music
  • Phrases should feel expansive and searching — not compact; Yaman breathes deeply
  • It is the emperor of the Kalyan family — majestic and comprehensive

12Yaman and the Kalyan Family
Yaman (pure)
.N R G m P D N S'
Only teevra Ma. Vadi: Ga. Samvadi: Ni. Shuddha Ma forbidden. Evening. The "emperor of Kalyan."
Yaman Kalyan
Same + shuddha Ma cadences
Adds shuddha Ma as relief/arrival (G Ma♮ G R S). Most performed concert form. Parrikar notes this is what most artists mean by "Yaman."
Bhupali
S R G P D S' (no Ma, no Ni)
Pentatonic cousin. Kalyan thaat. Vadi: Ga (same). Evening. Serene and simpler. Omits Ma entirely — contrast with Yaman's teevra Ma as defining feature.
Kedar
S M m P D N S'
Both Ma variants. Vadi: shuddha Ma (opposite to Yaman where Ga is vadi). Highly vakra. Evening. The defining meend D P m P M distinguishes it.
Kalyani (Carnatic)
S R2 G3 M2 P D2 N3 S'
The exact Carnatic equivalent of Yaman — 65th Melakarta. Same scale; different ornament system, gamaka, and emotional treatment.
Yaman vs Kalyan (older thaat)
Kalyan starts S R G
Kalyan uses S R G in aaroh; Yaman opens from .N R G. Most film songs called "Yaman" use S R G and are technically Kalyan. The .N opening distinguishes pure Yaman.
13Rasa aur Bhaav (रस और भाव)
Shringaar — romantic grandeur
The primary rasa — not the aching separation of Bageshri or the folk festivity of Kafi, but a grand, luminous romantic longing. Vast and reaching, like the evening sky itself expanding into night.
Shant — serene elevation
The teevra Ma creates a floating, suspended quality — serene but always reaching. The raga's calm is not passive but expansive: the peace of open sky, not closed room.
Bhakti — devotional surrender
"When we use teevra Ma, it is like a prayer of shantha" (Vilayat Khan). The sharpened fourth creates an upward pull — spiritually, this is the quality of prayer: reaching beyond the natural toward the divine.
Prakriti — the golden hour
Yaman is dusk made music — the exact moment the sky turns from gold to indigo. Expansive, complete, and suffused with the bittersweet feeling of a perfect day ending.

Yaman is canonically the evening raga — first prahar of the night (6–9 PM). In practice it is considered suitable for all night performance: "Yaman ragas are considered suitable to play at any time of the day, but they are traditionally performed in the evening." The raga's mood — expansive, luminous, neither anxious nor sleepy — is perfectly mapped to the transition from day to night.

18Practice Schedule

Daily practice — 50 minutes

  • 0–5 min: Tanpura setup; practise lower Ni → Re → Ga opening sweep slowly
  • 5–12 min: Alankar 1 — core aaroh–avroh; teevra m♯ throughout; .Ni as starting note
  • 12–18 min: Alankars 2–4 — step, skip, trikhand; maintain teevra Ma; Ga as resting note
  • 18–28 min: Alaap phrases 1–3; .N R G opening; Ga as primary nyaas; G m G cell
  • 28–38 min: Alaap phrases 4–6; taar saptak; Yaman Kalyan shuddha Ma cadence
  • 38–44 min: Sargam geet with Teentaal
  • 44–50 min: G m G signature taans; then Yaman Kalyan cadential taan

19Common Mistakes to Avoid
MistakeCorrection
Using komal Ma (F♮) as a resting noteIn pure Yaman, shuddha/komal Ma is strictly forbidden as a resting or prominent note. It belongs to the Kafi and Bilawal families. In Yaman Kalyan, shuddha Ma appears only in the G Ma♮ G R S cadence — never as an independent resting point.
Beginning the aaroh from SaYaman's ascending line must open from lower Ni (.Ni → Re → Ga). Beginning from Sa sounds like a scale, not Yaman. The .N R G sweep is the raga's melodic signature. Make it the first thing you practise every session.
Not dwelling on Ga (the vadi)Ga is the vadi — the emotional anchor. Phrases must resolve to, circle around, and linger on Ga. Treating Ga as a passing note denudes the raga of its identity. Apply meend and subtle andolan when resting on Ga.
Neglecting the G m G phrase cellThe G m G pattern — Ga both sides of teevra Ma — is the single most identifying melodic cell of Yaman. Not using it makes the raga sound like a generic Lydian scale. Practise it before anything else.
Confusing with Yaman KalyanPure Yaman has only teevra Ma. Yaman Kalyan adds shuddha Ma as a cadential arrival note (G Ma♮ G R S). Both are valid. Know which you are performing, and be consistent within a performance.
Confusing Yaman with Kalyan (film songs)Most Hindi film songs called "Yaman" use the older Kalyan form: ascending from Sa Re Ga (not .Ni Re Ga). The .Ni Re Ga opening distinguishes pure Yaman. Not all "Yaman" songs are in the classical Yaman raga.
Rushing past teevra MaThe teevra Ma (F♯) is the crown of Yaman — it must be sung with clarity and emotional sensitivity, not slid through quickly. It creates the floating, suspended quality of the raga. Rushing it collapses the raga's expansive feeling.
